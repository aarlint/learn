🌱 Parts of a Plant

Every plant has important parts that help it survive and grow!

🌿 Roots
Absorb water and nutrients from the soil. They anchor the plant in the ground.
🌿 Stem
Carries water and nutrients between roots and leaves. Supports the plant.
🍃 Leaves
Make food for the plant using sunlight, water, and air (photosynthesis!).
🌸 Flower
Makes seeds so new plants can grow. Attracts pollinators like bees!

🐾 Animal Classification

Scientists group animals based on their characteristics. Here are the main groups of vertebrates (animals with backbones):

🐟 Fish
Live in water, breathe with gills, have scales and fins. Cold-blooded.
🐸 Amphibians
Start life in water, move to land. Moist skin. Cold-blooded. (Frogs, salamanders)
🦎 Reptiles
Dry, scaly skin. Lay eggs on land. Cold-blooded. (Snakes, lizards, turtles)
🐦 Birds
Have feathers, wings, and beaks. Lay eggs. Warm-blooded.
🐕 Mammals
Have fur/hair, feed babies milk. Warm-blooded. (Dogs, whales, humans!)
🐛 Invertebrates
No backbone! (Insects, spiders, jellyfish, worms) — the biggest group!

🌤️ Weather vs. Climate

Weather is what's happening outside RIGHT NOW (sunny, rainy, snowy). It changes every day!

Climate is the USUAL weather in a place over a long time. Arizona has a hot, dry climate. Alaska has a cold climate.

☀️ Temperature
How hot or cold the air is. Measured in degrees (°F or °C).
💧 Precipitation
Water falling from the sky: rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
💨 Wind
Moving air. Can be calm, breezy, or very strong (hurricanes!).
☁️ Clouds
Made of tiny water droplets. Different shapes tell us about weather!

☁️ Cloud Types

Cumulus ☁️
White, fluffy, like cotton balls. Usually mean fair weather!
Stratus 🌫️
Flat, gray layers covering the sky. Can bring drizzle or light rain.
Cirrus 🪶
Thin, wispy, high up in the sky. Made of ice crystals!
Cumulonimbus ⛈️
Tall, dark storm clouds. Bring thunderstorms, heavy rain, even hail!

🔬 The Scientific Method

Scientists follow steps to learn about the world. These steps help them figure things out in an organized way!

1
Ask a Question — Wonder about something! "Why do plants lean toward the window?"
2
Make a Hypothesis — Your best guess! "I think plants lean toward light because they need it to grow."
3
Experiment — Test your guess! Put one plant by a window and one in a dark closet.
4
Observe & Record — Watch what happens and write it down! Measure, draw, take notes.
5
Draw a Conclusion — Was your hypothesis right? What did you learn?
