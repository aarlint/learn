🐕 Max's Big Day
Max was a small brown dog who lived with the Garcia family in a cozy house near the park. One sunny Saturday morning, Max slipped through an open gate and ran to the park. He chased squirrels, splashed in puddles, and made friends with a big golden retriever named Sunny. But when the sky got dark and thunder rumbled, Max got scared. He couldn't find his way home! Luckily, Sunny's owner saw Max's collar tag and called the Garcias. Mr. Garcia drove to the park and scooped Max into his arms. Max licked his face and wagged his tail all the way home. From that day on, the Garcias always double-checked the gate!

💡 What Is the Main Idea?

The main idea is what the passage is mostly about. It's the most important point the author is making. Details are the smaller facts that support the main idea.

Tip: Ask yourself: "If I could only say ONE thing about this passage, what would it be?"

🔗 Cause & Effect

Cause = WHY something happened. Effect = WHAT happened.

Look for signal words: because, so, since, therefore, as a result, due to

Cause (Why?)
It rained all day.
Effect (What?)
The soccer game was cancelled.

🔍 Making Inferences

An inference is a smart guess based on clues in the text plus what you already know. The author doesn't tell you directly — you figure it out!

Text Clues + What You Already Know = Inference
